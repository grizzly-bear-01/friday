The Department of Justice (DOJ) is a federal executive department of the U.S. government responsible for enforcing the law and defending the interests of the United States according to the law and regulations.

Finding Rules and Regulations

The United States Code is a compilation of most public laws currently in force, organized by subject matter into 50 titles. The U.S. Code collates the original law with subsequent amendments, and it deletes language that has later been repealed or superseded. Compilations of laws are collections of individual acts in their current, amended form with cross-references to the United States Code for ease of use. Legislative measures that have been introduced in Congress but have not become law are known as bills.

Business Law

Business law deals with the creation, management of businesses, as well as any legal issues that may arise in the course of their operations.

Travel Law

Travel law pertains to the rights and responsibilities of travelers, including issues related to visas, passport, and immigration.

Marriage Law

Marriage law regulates the legal requirements for getting married and the rights and responsibilities of married couples.

Drug Law

Drug law regulates the possession, use, and distribution of drugs, as well as penalties for drug offenses.

Gun Law

Gun law regulates the possession, use, and sale of firearms, as well as penalties for gun offenses. Using unlicensed gun is bad.

Money Laundering Law

Money laundering law is a criminal offense and it refers to process of disguising the proceeds of illegal activities as legitimate funds.

Flexbox and Grid are both layout systems in CSS that allow you to control the position and size of elements on a web page. However, they have different strengths and are best suited for different types of layout tasks.

Flexbox is a one-dimensional layout system, meaning that it can be used to lay out items in a single row or column. It is best suited for creating flexible and responsive layouts with items that need to be aligned along a single axis, such as a horizontal navigation bar or a vertical list of items. Flexbox makes it easy to distribute items evenly within a container and control the size and position of items based on the available space.

Grid, on the other hand, is a two-dimensional layout system, meaning that it can be used to create grid-based layouts with rows and columns. It is best suited for creating complex, multi-dimensional layouts with items that need to be aligned along both the x and y axis, such as a photo grid or a complex dashboard. The grid system allows you to create fixed and fluid grid layout, and you can control the size and position of items based on both the row and the column.

Media queries are a CSS feature that allows you to apply different styles to a webpage based on the characteristics of the device or screen displaying the webpage. This is particularly useful for creating responsive designs that adjust to different screen sizes, resolutions, and orientations.

The purpose of Media Query is to provide a way to apply styles based on the media type and media characteristics of the device that is viewing the website, Media queries use a combination of media type, such as screen or print, and media characteristics, such as screen width and resolution, to apply styles conditionally. This allows you to create designs that adapt to different devices and screen sizes, providing an optimal viewing experience for users.

The CSS box model is the concept that defines the layout of elements in a web page. It describes how elements are sized and positioned, and how they interact with other elements on the page. The box model is made up of several different parts:

  • Content: This is the area that contains the actual content of the element, such as text or images.
  • Padding: This is the space between the content and the border.
  • Border: This is the line that surrounds the content and padding.
  • Margin: This is the space between the border and the other elements on the page.

When you set the width and height properties of an element, you are setting the width and height of the content box. However, the total size of the element, as it appears on the page, is the sum of the content box, padding, border, and margin. This can be a source of confusion, as the element may appear larger or smaller than expected.

The box-sizing property can be used to change the way the box model works. The default value is "content-box", which means that the width and height properties apply only to the content box. However, you can also set the value to "border-box", which means that the width and height properties include the padding and border, so the total size of the element is the value you set.

A semantic tag is a label or marker that is added to a piece of content (such as text, audio, or video) to indicate its meaning or purpose. The tag can be used to classify the content, to indicate its relevance to a particular topic or theme, or to provide additional information about the content. Semantic tags can be used to improve the organization and searchability of content, and can also be used to enable automated processing of content (such as natural language understanding or image recognition).

The right to live, also known as the right to life, is the basic human right to exist and to have one's physical and mental integrity protected. It is the most fundamental of all human rights and is protected by various international and domestic laws. It encompasses the right to be free from arbitrary deprivation of life, including extrajudicial killings, genocide, and euthanasia. It also includes the right to live in a safe and healthy environment, the right to access to food, water, and health care, as well as the right to be free from torture and other forms of cruel, inhumane, or degrading treatment or punishment.
